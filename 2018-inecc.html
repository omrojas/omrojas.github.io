National Institute of Climate Change: Dynamic Website

Web application developed in Mexico, aimed at fostering connections between caretakers and elderly individuals in need of assistance. This platform serves as a trusted bridge, ensuring that seniors receive the personalized support they deserve while providing caretakers with meaningful employment opportunities.

  • User Research
  • Information Architecture
  • Wireframing

1. Strategy

Defining the requirements and functionalities of the product.

References

I defined the requirements using the HEART matrix, which is a framework that identifies the quality of the user experience. It stands for Happiness, Engagement, Adoption, Retention and Task Success. All of these are actually user metrics that cover key product aspects. HEART was conceived by Kerry Rodden, Hillary Hutchinson and Xin Fu to address the need for defining large-scale user-centred attitudinal and behavioural metrics and was initially applied to analyzing user experience related to websites and web applications

Mexico's and UK's official government websites, and NASA's official website.

Personas

Personas are a critical method for orienting design and development teams to user experience. They also provide a conduit for conveying a broad range of qualitative and quantitative data, and focus attention on aspects of design and use that other methods do not.

Illustration of the three personas I created in Figma.

2. Scope

Defining the requirements and functionalities of the project.

HEART Matrix

The HEART framework allowed me to evaluate user experience quality through key metrics: Happiness, Engagement, Adoption, Retention, and Task Success. Developed by Kerry Rodden, Hillary Hutchinson, and Xin Fu, HEART addresses the need for large-scale, user-centered metrics for websites and web applications.

3. Structure

Outlining the information architecture and interaction design.

Journey Maps

The reasons for creating customer journey maps include understanding the path and channels of the users take to a product. It's a valuable tool that can be used to forecast the path of future users as well.

Illustration of each persona's journey maps. Made in Figma. ®.

Sitemap

A sitemap is a diagrammatic representation of a hierarchical system. It usually depicts the parent-sibling relationship between pages in a website, showing how sub pages might be arranged underneath their parent groupings. This arrangement forms a map of the site.

Sitemap with the titles of each webpage in Spanish, of course.

4. Skeleton

Describing the wireframes, layout, and navigational elements.

Wireframes

They’re purposely inelegant yet they’re descriptive. Within minutes, user-journeys can be visualised to a wider team opening channels of collaboration and understanding. Understanding low-fi mockups require an inherent understanding of GUI design.

Wireframes I made in Marvel App. My very first design tool.

Conclusion

Summarizing the project outcomes and key learnings.

Outcomes

The strategic objective of Mexico's climate change website is to disseminate, promote, and spread useful and accurate information about this challenge. Making this information easy to find and discoverable is critical to its mission. In addition to the site's dissemination strategies through media by the social communication department, the following actions are proposed as part of an organic dissemination plan for the site's content.

Search Engine Optimization (SEO) The main source of organic traffic to the website is web search engines, particularly Google, which has a penetration rate of over 90% in Mexico.

Social Media: Social media platforms play a significant role in promoting the website, as they are the second most important source of traffic for the site. The graph below shows the most popular social media platforms in Mexico over the past year.

Other Media The digital ecosystem in which Mexico's climate change website operates consists of several communication channels like email, video, search engines, displays and mobile devices.
