Nina Florería: Web E-commerce

Nina Fleurs et Déco is a company originally from the city of Querétaro, they offer as part of their services the sale of floral arrangements and different seasonal gifts. Due to the pandemic caused by COVID-19, the Medina Preciado family decided to obtain a website where they could sell their services, in addition to knowing in depth their identity and values ​​that stand out to them.

1. Requirements Elicitation

The Software Requirements Specification (ERS) is a complete description of the behavior of the system to be developed. Includes a set of use cases that describe all the interactions users will have with the software.

MVP

Nina Florería wants to make its value proposition known through the brand image and design, emulate the experience of a physical store within the website and sell its product catalog through the website.

Initial Release Features

Personas are a critical method for orienting design and development teams to user experience. They also provide a conduit for conveying a broad range of qualitative and quantitative data, and focus attention on aspects of design and use that other methods do not.

Feature 1 (FE-1)

The user will be able to get to know Nina Fleurs et Déco's value proposition in the first instance through its Landing Page where he/she can intuitively navigate through the rest of the site.

Feature 2 (FE-2)

You will be able to select your preferred products within a digital shopping cart while the cart displays the total amount of products.

Feature 3 (FE-3)

When a user is satisfied with their items, they can proceed to checkout, where they can pay for them and place their order with the necessary shipping address and personalisation.

Feature 4 (FE-4)

Un usuario podrá navegar en las distintas páginas de ninafloreria.com, en donde podrá encontrar una serie de información pertinente como cobertura de envíos, FAQ, logística de entregas, cuidado de sus arreglos.

Feature 5 (FE-5)

The website shall contain an approved design and iconography.

Feature 6 (FE-)

A user can subscribe to a newsletter to receive updates about the business, trends, important dates and promotions.

Prototypes are a limited representation of a product, allowing parties to test it in real-life situations or explore its use, thus creating an iterative design process that generates quality. A prototype can be anything from a piece of paper with simple drawings to complex software..

Usability Tests

Usability tests are a fundamental component of user-centered design, providing invaluable insights into how real users interact with a product. By observing users as they perform specific tasks, designers can identify pain points, uncover usability issues, and gather qualitative feedback that highlights areas for improvement.
